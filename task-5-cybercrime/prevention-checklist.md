# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | CSE0001 Digital Literacy

---

## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords.**
   Create passwords with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g. R@in#2025). Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).**
   Turn on 2FA for email, banking apps, and social media. Even if your password is stolen, your account stays protected.

3. **Never click unknown links.**
   Do not click links in SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown senders. These are the most common phishing delivery methods.

4. **Verify before you trust.**
   If someone calls claiming to be from your bank, college, or government — hang up and call back on the official published number to verify their identity.

5. **Keep your phone and apps updated.**
   Regularly update your operating system and installed apps to patch known security vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.

6. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.**
   Never log in to banking apps, college portals, or email on unverified public Wi-Fi — use mobile data instead.

---

## Financial / UPI Safety

7. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money.**
   Your UPI PIN is ONLY required to SEND money. If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to receive a payment, refund, or prize — it is a SCAM. Decline immediately and block the contact.

8. **Always verify Collect Requests before approving.**
   Check the full name and UPI ID of whoever sent the Collect Request. If you do not personally know the sender, REJECT it. Approving an unverified Collect Request debits your account instantly and irreversibly.

---

## Reporting Channels

9. **Report cybercrime immediately at cybercrime.gov.in.**
   File a complaint at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal as soon as possible. Early reporting increases the chance of freezing the fraudster's account before money is moved further.

10. **Call Helpline 1930 for financial cyber fraud.**
    Dial **1930** (National Cyber Crime Helpline, 24x7, Government of India) the moment you suspect financial fraud. Keep the fraudster's number and UPI ID ready when you call.
