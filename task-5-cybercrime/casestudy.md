# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud
**Region:** India (college students)
**Source:** cybercrime.gov.in

---

## What Is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a category of financial cybercrime in which attackers deceive victims into authorising fraudulent transactions or disclosing sensitive payment credentials. Unlike traditional hacking, UPI fraud relies almost entirely on social engineering — manipulating human behaviour rather than breaking technical systems. In India, billions of rupees are lost annually to this crime, with college students and first-time digital payment users among the most frequent targets.

---

## How It Happens — Step by Step

**Step 1 — Target identification**
The fraudster identifies a potential victim through social media, classified ad platforms (such as OLX or Facebook Marketplace), or cold calls. Students selling second-hand items, or those who have recently posted about financial needs, are common targets.

**Step 2 — Impersonation and trust-building**
The attacker poses as a genuine buyer, a bank representative, a government official, or even a fellow student. They communicate politely and professionally to establish trust before making any payment-related request.

**Step 3 — Creating urgency**
The victim is told they have received a payment, must claim a prize or cashback, or need to verify their account urgently. This pressure is deliberate — it prevents the victim from pausing to verify the situation.

**Step 4 — The Collect Request trap**
The fraudster sends a UPI "Collect Money" request — NOT a payment. The victim is told to "approve the incoming payment" by entering their UPI PIN. However, entering a PIN on a Collect Request authorises a DEBIT from the victim's account, not a credit.

**Step 5 — Instant transfer and disappearance**
The money is transferred instantly to the fraudster's account and often moved through multiple hops to avoid tracing. The attacker immediately blocks the victim and becomes unreachable. UPI transactions are irreversible once completed.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

| Who | Why they are vulnerable |
|-----|------------------------|
| College students | First-time UPI users with limited fraud awareness |
| Online sellers | People listing items on OLX, Quikr, or Facebook Marketplace |
| Senior citizens | Less familiar with digital payment flows and Collect Requests |
| Job seekers | Targeted with fake "advance payment for job offer" scams |

---

## Consequences

- **Financial loss** — Money is transferred instantly and is nearly impossible to recover once moved.
- **Emotional distress** — Victims report anxiety, shame, and loss of trust in digital payment systems.
- **Academic disruption** — For students, unexpected financial loss can affect fees, books, and daily needs.
- **Systemic impact** — India loses billions annually to UPI fraud, eroding trust in digital payments nationwide.
