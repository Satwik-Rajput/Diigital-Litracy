# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's
1. Keep your profiles professional — use a clear, appropriate display photo and a bio that reflects your academic interests and goals.
2. Think before you post — ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.
3. Review your privacy settings regularly on every platform to control who can see your posts, location, and personal details.
4. Credit original creators when sharing or reposting content — always tag or mention the source.
5. Build your professional brand on LinkedIn — share certifications, projects, and academic milestones noticed by recruiters.
6. Fact-check before sharing any news or claim on a trusted source.
7. Engage positively and constructively — comment with respect, encouragement, or genuine questions.

## DON'Ts
1. Don't share personal details (phone number, home address, daily schedule) publicly where strangers can see them.
2. Don't post complaints about faculty or college on public platforms — even casual venting can reach faculty and have serious academic consequences.
3. Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling, even "as a joke" — it can lead to disciplinary action and legal consequences.
4. Don't accept connection requests from strangers without first verifying their profile.
5. Don't overshare personal updates, especially on LinkedIn, which is viewed by recruiters and potential employers.
6. Don't use your college email for general social media sign-ups — keep institutional email for academic and professional platforms only.
7. Don't post during exams, placements, or other sensitive academic periods — inappropriate posts can seriously affect your reputation.
