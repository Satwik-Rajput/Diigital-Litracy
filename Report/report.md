This project report documents the work completed as part of the **CSE0001 — Digital Literacy** course at VIT Bhopal University. ## Task 1 — Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

### Tool Used  
Canva (canva.com) — a free browser-based design platform that provides drag-and-drop tools, templates, icons, and layout grids ideal for infographic creation.

### What the Design Shows  
I created a one-page infographic titled **“Digital Literacy for Students.”** The infographic is organised into five clearly labelled sections using distinct colour bands, relevant icons, and concise bullet points:

- **What is Digital Literacy** — The ability to use digital tools effectively, safely, and responsibly in academic, personal, and professional contexts.

- **Useful Digital Tools for Students** — Tools highlighted include Google Docs (collaborative writing), Canva (design), Notion (note-taking), Grammarly (writing support), and Google Meet (virtual collaboration).

- **Safe Internet Practices** — Covered strong passwords, enabling Two-Factor Authentication, identifying phishing links, and avoiding public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities.

- **Professional Online Presence** — Emphasised the importance of LinkedIn, GitHub, and Kaggle for career development, along with tips on maintaining professional profiles.

- **Email Etiquette** — Summarised essential rules such as clear subject lines, professional greetings, structured content, and proper sign-offs.

### One Difficulty / Interesting Part  
The most interesting challenge was fitting five comprehensive topics into a single page without clutter. I carefully planned the visual hierarchy — deciding which information should stand out, what could be represented with icons, and how to maintain sufficient whitespace. Canva’s grid and alignment tools were helpful, but multiple revisions were required to balance detail with readability. This task taught me that effective design is not only about aesthetics but also about presenting information clearly and efficiently.

## Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide

### Topic: UPI / Online Payment Fraud  
UPI fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. Fraudsters impersonate trusted individuals, create urgency, and send fraudulent “Collect Money” requests. Victims mistakenly enter their UPI PIN, unknowingly authorising money transfers.

### Prevention Checklist Summary  
Key safety measures include:

- Never enter UPI PIN to receive money  
- Verify collect requests and UPI IDs carefully  
- Use strong passwords and enable 2FA  
- Avoid phishing links and unsecured Wi-Fi  
- Report incidents at cybercrime.gov.in or Helpline 1930  

### What Surprised Me Most  
I was surprised that most UPI scams rely on psychological manipulation rather than technical hacking. Even educated users fall victim due to urgency and misplaced trust.

### One Habit I Will Change  
I will verify all UPI requests thoroughly before approval and educate family members about safe digital payment practices.
